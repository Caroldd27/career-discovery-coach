# Deliverables — templates for the "Activate" movement

Once the bedrock is clear, turn insight into things the person can *use*. Tailor
everything to the psychology you uncovered.

## 1. Personality portrait
A short synthesis said back as a *person*, not a trait list. 4-6 lines covering:
motivation type, maker archetype, competitive orientation, the mission anchor, and
the one reframed "flaw → fit signal." End with the uncomfortable truth (e.g., the
credential–wiring mismatch). This is the emotional payoff — the "that's me, and I
never saw it" moment. Earn it before mapping careers.

## 2. Positioning one-liner + differentiators
A single sentence the person can use in their profile, intro, and résumé:

> "A [domain-depth] who also [rare second strength] and has [proof of a third] —
> i.e. a [crisp identity] for [target arena]."

Then **3 differentiators** in a small table: each strength + *what a hiring
reader infers from it*. The power is usually in the *combination* most peers can't
match, not any single line.

## 3. Career lane map
2-3 lanes, each scored against the person's stated constraints (growth, pay,
employability/difficulty, geography/visa). Use a table:

| Lane | What it is | Fit with their wiring | Pay / growth | How realistic (constraints) |

Rules: name a clear **top recommendation**; **kill** lanes they've ruled out;
**sequence** launchpad → destination ("the realistic first job that buys you X,
on the way to the thing you actually want"). Recommend — don't leave them a menu.

## 4. Profile / résumé reframe (if relevant)
Principles, not just edits:
- **Formality comes from strong verbs + quantified results + relevance framing +
  mirroring the target's vocabulary — not from the topic being "serious."** A side
  hustle described with operator verbs and hard numbers reads as premium signal;
  the same thing described as a "hobby" reads as frivolous.
- **Quantify everything** (units, %, growth, scale). Numbers make anything credible.
- **Tailor weight to audience:** lead with the asset that audience values; demote
  the rest. Often one experience needs two differently-weighted versions for two
  kinds of roles.
- **Surface hidden differentiators** the person buried as "just a hobby" — these
  are often their actual moat.

## 5. Activation plan with anti-procrastination mechanism
A time-boxed plan (e.g., 90 days) with a weekly cadence and quota. The
**mechanism must match their motivation type:**

- **Externally-triggered →** build in *external* structure: public progress posts
  (turn their audience-need into a tool), an accountability partner, a visible
  tracker, fixed weekly quotas with a real deadline. Tell them plainly: your
  willpower isn't the lever — your environment is.
- **Autonomy-driven →** give them ownership of the design; let them set the
  targets so it's theirs.
- **Arena-driven →** add a scoreboard / friendly competition.

Always include realistic **funnel math** so normal rejection doesn't read as
failure ("apply ~N → interview ~M → offer 1-3; your KPI is the *input* quota you
control, not today's outcome"). Convert relative dates to absolute. Leave them
with a tiny, concrete first action to beat inertia.
